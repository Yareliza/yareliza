About Yareliza

My name is Yarelis Cristina Peña Almonte.

My grandmother Mercedes was the only one who called me Yareliza.
Today I carry that name on stage, in her honor.

She gave me my first turquoise notebook when I was eleven, with one rule: "the words come first, mija. the voice comes after." She was right. I filled nine notebooks before I ever sang a single note.

When she passed in 2025, I decided to sing.

I was born in Santiago and raised in Tamboril. I left the Dominican Republic at three years old and grew up in Washington Heights, between two countries, two languages.

Today I make alternative rap and R&B in spanglish.
It's not radio american/latin pop.

It's who I am.

This album lived quietly in my notebooks.
Now I'm turning it into my debut album.

The one she'll never hear, but the one she made possible.

Four names. One voice.
